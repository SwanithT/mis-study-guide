Chapter 5: Zara & Supply Chain

How supply chains, information systems, and strategy create competitive advantage.

---

Vocabulary

Supply Chain: A network of organizations involved in producing and delivering a product.
Example: Suppliers → manufacturers → distributors → retailers → customers.
Supply Chain Management (SCM): Coordinating activities across firms to deliver value efficiently.
Example: Zara tracking demand and adjusting production quickly.
Vertical Integration: When a firm owns multiple stages of its supply chain.
Example: Zara controlling design, manufacturing, and distribution.
Just-in-Time (JIT): Producing goods only when needed to reduce inventory.
Example: Zara producing small batches frequently.
Inventory Turnover: How quickly inventory is sold and replaced.
Example: Zara sells items quickly to avoid excess stock.
Bullwhip Effect: Small demand changes cause larger fluctuations up the supply chain.
Example: Retailers over-order due to perceived shortages.
Disintermediation: Removing intermediaries from the supply chain.
Example: Dell selling directly to customers.
Lead Time: Time between ordering and receiving a product.
Example: Zara’s lead time is ~15 days.
Contract Manufacturing: Outsourcing production to third-party firms.
Example: Many apparel brands outsource manufacturing.
Core Competency: What a company does best that gives it advantage.
Example: Zara’s fast fashion responsiveness.
