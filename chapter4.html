Chapter 4: Disruptive Technologies

How new technologies reshape industries and why big firms often fail to adapt.

---

Vocabulary

Disruptive Innovation: A new technology or approach that starts off worse in traditional metrics but eventually replaces existing solutions.
Example: Netflix replacing Blockbuster.
Sustaining Innovation: Improvements to existing products for current customers.
Example: New iPhone versions with better cameras.
Enabling Technology: A technology that makes products more affordable and accessible to a larger population.
Example: Cloud computing enabling SaaS products.
Innovative Business Model: A new way of delivering or pricing a product that attracts new or low-end users.
Example: Ride-sharing being cheaper than taxis.
Value Network: The ecosystem of suppliers, distributors, and customers that support a product.
Example: Electric cars + charging stations.
Technology Price Elasticity: When lower prices dramatically increase demand.
Example: Cheap streaming increasing video consumption.
Analog to Digital Shift: Moving from physical formats to digital formats.
Example: DVDs → streaming.
Long Tail: Selling many niche products instead of just a few popular ones.
Example: Netflix offering thousands of less popular shows.
First-Mover Advantage: Gaining advantage by entering a market early.
Example: Netflix in streaming.
Incumbent Firm: An established company currently dominating a market.
Example: Blockbuster before Netflix.
