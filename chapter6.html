Chapter 6: Moore’s Law & Hardware

Key concepts, definitions, and scenario-based questions.

---

Vocabulary

Moore’s Law: The observation that computing power increases exponentially over time as the number of transistors on a chip doubles roughly every 18–24 months.
Example: AI tools today are much faster and cheaper than just a few years ago.
Price Elasticity (Technology): As computing becomes cheaper, demand increases because people find new ways to use it.
Example: Lower cloud storage costs led companies to store massive amounts of data.
CPU: The component that processes instructions and performs calculations.
Example: A faster CPU helps programs run quicker.
Memory (RAM): Temporary working space used to hold data while programs are running.
Example: More RAM allows you to run multiple apps smoothly.
Storage: Long-term data storage that keeps information even when power is off.
Example: Files and apps saved on your laptop stay after shutdown.
Latency: The delay before data begins to transfer after a request is made.
Example: Lag in online games is caused by high latency.
Bandwidth: The amount of data that can be transferred in a given time.
Example: Streaming video requires high bandwidth.
Konana’s Software Ecosystem: A layered system where each level depends on the one below, creating lock-in and making switching difficult.
Example: Apps, operating systems, and hardware working together keep users in one ecosystem.
e-Waste: Discarded electronics that create environmental and health risks.
Example: Old phones and laptops thrown away contribute to pollution.
