Chapter 2: Strategy and Technology

Competitive advantage, industry structure, and strategic thinking.

---

Vocabulary

Competitive Advantage (CA): The ability of a firm to consistently outperform competitors.
Example: Apple charging premium prices due to brand and ecosystem.
Sustainable Competitive Advantage: A long-term advantage that competitors cannot easily replicate.
Example: Amazon’s logistics network.
Strategy: Choosing a unique set of activities to deliver value differently than competitors.
Example: Southwest focusing on low-cost, short flights.
Porter’s Five Forces: A model analyzing industry competition and profitability.
Example: High rivalry lowers profits in the airline industry.
Rivalry: Competition among existing firms.
Example: Airlines competing on price.
Threat of New Entrants: How easy it is for new competitors to enter a market.
Example: Tech startups entering app markets easily.
Threat of Substitutes: Availability of alternative products.
Example: Uber replacing taxis.
Bargaining Power of Suppliers: Ability of suppliers to influence prices.
Example: Chip manufacturers controlling supply.
Bargaining Power of Buyers: Ability of customers to influence prices.
Example: Large companies negotiating bulk discounts.
Barriers to Entry: Obstacles that make it difficult for new firms to enter a market.
Example: High startup costs in airlines.
Switching Costs: Costs of changing from one product or service to another.
Example: Switching from iPhone to Android.
Network Effects: Value increases as more people use a product.
Example: Instagram becomes more valuable as more users join.
Value Chain: The set of activities used to create and deliver a product or service.
Example: Production → marketing → delivery → service.
Primary Activities: Activities directly involved in creating and delivering a product.
Example: Manufacturing and sales.
Support Activities: Activities that support primary activities.
Example: HR and IT.
Business Process: A set of steps used to complete a business task.
Example: Processing an online order.
