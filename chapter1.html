Chapter 1: Setting the Stage

Core concepts for how people, thinking, and technology interact.

---

Vocabulary

Self-Directed Learning: The ability to teach yourself by finding and evaluating information independently.
Example: Googling Excel functions and figuring them out without a professor.
Mental Model: A person’s internal way of understanding how the world works, shaped by experience and background.
Example: Someone who grew up using Macs thinking all computers should work the same way.
Curse of Knowledge: When someone who knows a lot about a topic struggles to explain it to beginners.
Example: A professor skipping steps because they assume students already understand basics.
Information System (IS): A system that collects, processes, and communicates information.
Example: Canvas showing assignments and grades.
Business Process: A set of steps used to complete a task or achieve a goal in a business.
Example: Ordering, shipping, and delivering a product.
Technology Hype Cycle: A model describing how new technologies go through hype, disappointment, and eventual usefulness.
Example: AI getting overhyped, then criticized, then actually becoming useful.
Peak of Inflated Expectations: Stage where a technology is overhyped and expectations are unrealistic.
Example: People thinking AI will replace all jobs immediately.
Trough of Disillusionment: Stage where people lose interest because the technology didn’t meet expectations.
Example: Companies abandoning early AI projects.
Slope of Enlightenment: Stage where realistic use cases and benefits begin to emerge.
Example: Companies using AI for specific tasks like automation.
Plateau of Productivity: Stage where the technology becomes widely adopted and useful.
Example: Cloud computing becoming standard.
Actual Demand: Real usage of a product based on real customers.
Example: People actually paying for a product.
Perceived Demand: What companies think customers want.
Example: A company thinking AI demand is huge based on hype.
Anticipatory Demand: Investments based on future expectations rather than current usage.
Example: Investors funding startups expecting future growth.
Technology Adoption: The process of people and companies starting to use a new technology.
Example: Students switching from Excel to Google Sheets.
